Year 9 Homework: Create a 20–30 second video advert

Create a short advert using Canva Video for one of these: a food chain restaurant, a soft drink, or a pair of trainers.

Open Canva Planning Sheet Marking Rubric

Tip: Get the first draft done quickly, then improve it with polish and pacing.

The task (from the brief)

  • Length: 20–30 seconds
  • Format: video advert (Canva Video Editor)
  • Choose one: food chain restaurant / soft drink / trainers
  • Include key elements: brand name, product focus point, persuasive message
  • Use the skills learnt: trim/cut, timing, transitions, text, audio, exports

Choose ONE advert scenario (copy this brief)

Pick one of these three “client briefs” so your advert has a clear purpose and target audience.

Food chain

Burger Barn — After‑School Meal Deal

Launch a new Spicy Chicken Wrap Meal Deal aimed at Year 9–11 students on the way home. Promote price, taste, and limited‑time urgency.

Call to action: “Grab it today after school.”

Soft drink

ZEST! — Zero Sugar, Big Flavour

Promote a new zero‑sugar citrus fizz for teenagers who want something refreshing. Focus on flavour, cool visuals, and a simple slogan.

Call to action: “Crack open a ZEST! now.”

Trainers

AeroStep — Light. Fast. Everyday.

Advertise lightweight trainers designed for PE and weekends. Show comfort, grip, and style with quick action shots.

Call to action: “Step up — available now.”

You can invent your own brand name/logo if you want — but it must look consistent throughout the advert.

Success criteria

  • Clear brand identity (logo/brand name + consistent colours/fonts)
  • Strong product focus (what are you selling and why is it good?)
  • Call to action (e.g., “Try it today”, “Available now”, “Shop online”)
  • Good pacing (not rushed, not slow; readable text)
  • Audio choices that match the advert (music/voiceover/SFX)
  • Exported cleanly (no cut-off text, no missing audio, correct length)

Plan before you edit

Use the planning sheet (download above) or jot down:

  1. Product + brand: what are you advertising?
  2. Target audience: who is it for?
  3. Key message: what should the viewer remember?
  4. 3–6 scenes: quick storyboard (1–5 seconds each)
  5. Audio: music / voiceover / SFX (keep it appropriate)

How to build your advert in Canva

  1. In Canva, create a design: Video (or Mobile Video if you want portrait).
  2. Add your background (colour, gradient, image, or video clip).
  3. Add brand name + logo (or a simple logo you design).
  4. Build scenes using the timeline: trim, split, and adjust duration.
  5. Add text that is big enough and on screen long enough to read.
  6. Add audio (music or voiceover) and make sure it isn’t too loud vs speech.
  7. Export: Share → Download → MP4 Video. Check the length is 20–30 seconds.

Quality check: Watch it all the way through twice. Fix anything you notice.

How to share a Canva link (required)

You must submit a view link so your teacher can watch your advert in a browser.

  1. Open your video in Canva.
  2. Click Share (top-right).
  3. Choose Anyone with the link and set it to Can view (not edit).
  4. Click Copy link.
  5. Paste that link into the submission form below.

If your link doesn’t work, it usually means the permissions aren’t set to “anyone with the link can view”.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Text appears for too short to read.
  • No brand name or no clear product focus.
  • Too many effects/transitions (keep it clean).
  • Audio is too loud or doesn’t match the advert.
  • Export is the wrong length (must be 20–30 seconds).
  • Share link is not viewable (teacher can’t access it).

Submit your work

Example format: canva.com/design/.../watch or canva.com/design/.../view

What happens next?
